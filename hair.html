Health Measures

A primary goal of this study is to compare levels of stress between you and your child by examining the cortisol that can be measured in hair. By looking at this stress hormone among dyads, we hope to learn more about the two of you on a biological level! Please follow the steps described below for both you and your child and send your samples to the following address: Stacey Doan Claremont McKenna College 850 Columbia Ave. Claremont, CA 91711

What You Need

  • Scissors

  • Comb

  • Hair Clip

  • Floss or String

  • Aluminium Foil

How to Cut the Hair:

  • Starting at the end of each eyebrow, trace a line and separate hair so that it is divided into two large sections.

  • Use a hair clip to lift the top section of hair, revealing the midsection of the scalp.

  • Collect a bundle of hair strands of about 3 - 5mm, which is about half the diameter of a pencil.

  • Comb the strand.

  • Using a piece of floss, tie a double knot around the bundle about .5 inches away from the scalp.

  • Cut the hair as close as possible to the scalp above the knot. Keep the tie on the hair to indicate the scalp near end.

Handling Hair Sample

  • On the piece of aluminum foil, write “Root” with a black sharpie on the side that will line up with the double knot that is holding the sample.

  • Fold the aluminium foil without bending the hair.

  • Write your email address and the date of the clipping on the foil.

  • Place the aluminium foil in an envelope and also label your email address and today’s date on the front of the envelope.
